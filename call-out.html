Call Out

An app to simplify the process of connecting a service to a customer.

Roles

Concept creater, user research, user interviews, wireframing, prototyping, visual design and branding.

The problem

Searching online and then phoning for a service can take a lot of time and cause frustration, how can the process be simplified?

Search Engine

The majority of people looking for a service use an online search engine. This method will generally return multiple options with a wide ranging amount of data to filter resulting in multiple phone calls to secure a service.

Referral/Social Media

Referrals are usually undertaken using social media platforms or from friends and family. Face Book groups are a popular place to request referrals.

Findings

After searching online for providers, many phone calls may have to be made to find someone who can provide the required service in a certain time frame.

The best price and service may not be achieved within a search area, as not all businesses may be contacted, and consequently not given the opportunity to offer their services.

Users find filtering through search results overwhelming. Often they are presented with businesses that donâ€™t meet their requirements.

Stress caused from the frustration of searching for a service is a major consideration for users.

How might we

How might we help a user find the service or business with the least amount of stress?

Research

Simplifying the contact process

Learning that the actual process of searching and the way of communication with a business/service was key to the cause of frustration and stress for a user and was a pivotal movement for this project. This helped clarify a few key aspects of what the solution needed to address.

The power of targeted messaging

By sending a single message to multiple businesses at once, users can avoid many of the stressful aspects of searching for a service and then calling them until a solution is reached. This message could be broken down into the type of service, area required, timing and pricing meaning that only the relevant businesses recieve the message.

Many iterations of paper prorotypes were used to refine and solidify the key features.

Core functions

User feedback through interviews helped define the app's most essential features

Simple Steps

Users want a simple interface that is easy and quick to enter their information. They want an easy reply system once a service is found and a record of that conversation with the selected supplier.

Business use

For a business to consider implementing this app, it has to be able to run in the background of existing software so any office staff can answer messages quickly and consequently book the work.

Messaging communication

The younger generation are very comfortable with this messaging style of communication rather than phoning so were welcoming of this concept.

Businesses

The Business End

For this app to function it requires both the customer and the service/business provider to actively engage. Businesses spoken to said the concept is great and they can see real benefits for them. For example it would give them the option of vetting the client (star rating and reviews), they could pick and choose the work to fit within their schedule. Both small and large businesses are on an equal footing with this app. The interface they use would need to be able to be run in conjunction with other software and be simple to use for the staff overseeing the scheduling.

Summary..

For this app to achieve its prime objective of connecting people to business in an easy and in the least stressful way, it requires adherence from the user and the businesses that provide the service. For this to occur, the app requires a clear design, as few as possible steps to select the target service and the knowledge that the user will get a response. From the business providers end, they want it run in the background and not to interfere with current business software and an easy clear interface that alerts staff to incoming messages.

A potential rollout may be achieved with the New Zealand Motor Caravan Association (NZMCA). They have over 100k members that constantly require services in unfamiliar areas as they travel. From mechanical, electrical, refrigeration and even dog grooming and hair dressing services.

2020

Jmee Lee Makeup Artist

Website rebrand and redesign

Roles

User interviews, wireframing, prototyping, visual design and branding.

The brief

Rebrand and redesign an exisiting website

Key issues

What are the main issues with the current site?


  • Branding/colours not representative of the client.
  • Minimal call to action forms and buttons throughout.
  • Poor quality photos.
  • Layout requires improvement.

The site needs to convert website traffic into work for the client

The question

Customers problem

A potential customer visiting this website is looking for an answer to their question, and that is, does this makeup artist have the skills to meet my needs? This site needs to answer that quickly and secure a booking from the customer?

Solve the problem

The value proposition

For a customer to determine that Jmee Lee Makeup, can provide the service they require, they need to be convinced thoroughly before they take the next step of making that connection with her or else they move onto another provider. Testimonials and photographs are a convincing proof source that need prominence in this redesign.

Testimonials

Although the current site has these, it is important to increase the emphasis and add more. They prove she is reliable, has the personality to be able to work with them and has the skills to do the job

Photographs

High resolution, professional photographs of her work need to replace inferior pictures. These placed prominently on the landing page will attract a user deeper into the website, potentially resulting in a booking.

CTA

Call to action

Once the customer understands that their needs can be met by Jmee Lee Makeup, it is important to make it as simple as possible for them to take the next step, which is contact. These contact forms need to be placed at the top of pages along with further call to action buttons emphasizing the user to act now.

Brand, colour and typeface

Four key areas of change

Replace the logo with a less masculine image, change out colours with a more natural contrasting pallette, introduce a complimentary typeface and improve the overall layout design.

Logo

Combination of two classic typefaces, serif and script types, emphasising different layers, size and weights. A subtle pink background to contrast dark lettering.

Colour

Replace ‘shocking pink’ with an umber skin colour for a more natural tone. Strong, classic contrast with black type on a white background. Mid grey secondary type.

Fonts

Introduce a serif typeface for the h1 headings and a minimalist sans fontype to compliment.

Layout

Wireframing

Various UI designs were explored. An important factor was to showcase the photos in the best format and to include cta buttons and forms prominently on numerous pages.

Small screens

Mobile first

In a recent survey Jmee Lee Makeup undertook with her clients, the majority looked for her and booked from their mobile device. This accentuated the importance of the quality of her photographic portfolio. These needed to consist of high reolution professional pictures.
This mobile first approach also meant the font size, spacing etc played a key role in clarity of her site.
